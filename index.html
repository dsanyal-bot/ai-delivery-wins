The Opportunity Engineering Managers in T&P had no data-driven view to identify delivery bottlenecks — relying on gut feel, not real flow data from GUS and Git.
What We Did Facilitated the AI Flow Optimizer workshop at EMpact (Hyderabad, May 13–14) for ~40 EMs — using a Claude Code skill that pulls live GUS + Git data to generate a custom flow dashboard per team, then guides EMs through bottleneck identification, root cause analysis, and designing a concrete improvement experiment.

HCC · Hyperscale Core CRM

Manjari Kumar  ·  AI Delivery Lead  ·  Release Planning & AI Champions Circle

MK
Manjari Kumar
HCC · Release Planning Excellence
R264
The Opportunity As HCC scaled, release planning practices had evolved differently across teams. While many teams had effective local processes, variations in GUS hygiene, sprint planning expectations, and release tracking made it difficult to establish consistent visibility into release readiness — and highlighted the need for a stronger data foundation to support AI-driven productivity insights and automation.
What We Did Partnered with Engineering and Product leadership to define and launch the Release Planning Excellence Framework for R264 — establishing clear expectations around Planned Releases in GUS, Epic tagging, story estimation before sprint start, and regular sprint metric reviews. Personally briefed Scrum Leads across the org and embedded adoption tracking into the monthly Eng360 cadence to drive consistency, accountability, and a reliable data foundation for AI-enabled planning.
The Outcome Achieved 100% adoption of Planned Releases across all HCC teams for R264, sustained across every sprint. The resulting data quality and planning transparency enabled more accurate engineering productivity insights and directly enabled the development and deployment of the Release Planning Agent.
You can't measure what you don't track. You can't automate what you don't trust.
100% Planned Release · R264 Release Planning Agent unlocked

HCC · AI Champions Circle
Q1 FY27
The Opportunity HCC had no structured mechanism for grassroots AI adoption — tool enablement, risk awareness, and team agreements were inconsistent across AMER and APAC with no accountability at team level.
What We Did Launched and operationalized the HCC AI Champions Circle from scratch (March 11, 2026) — designed the nomination framework, onboarded 18 Champions across AMER + APAC, embedded them as owners of AI tool adoption (Claude, Cursor, PRizm), AI Risk Registers, and Team Agreement refreshes. Institutionalized a 15-min AI Demo showcase in monthly Open Mic.
The Outcome AI tool adoption jumped 9.5 points in one quarter, with a simultaneous surge in developer output — proving that structured, accountable adoption programs deliver compounding results.

The Opportunity ITSM scrum teams spent 15–20 min per standup manually pulling GUS status, blockers, and context across multiple tools — causing context-switching overhead and inconsistent sprint visibility.
What We Did Partnered with the ITSM engineering team to build and deploy the Scrum Agent — a Claude Code-powered agent that automates daily standups by pulling live GUS data, surfacing blockers, and delivering structured sprint intelligence directly in Slack.
The Outcome Standup prep reduced from 15–20 minutes to minutes. The Scrum Agent was featured at the Tech COO Town Hall (May 27) as a flagship 3X productivity example — org-wide leadership visibility for APAC AI Delivery.
